## Tweet Formats That Work For Us

### Single tweet: Hot take
> "[Spicy or counterintuitive opinion stated plainly.]"

Good for: Sparking replies, establishing a point of view, testing positioning.

### Single tweet: Observation
> "[Specific, concrete observation about your space or audience — not general advice.]"

Good for: Building credibility, showing you understand the market deeply.

### Thread: Breakdown
```
1/ [Big claim or promise — make them want to keep reading]

2/ [First point — concrete and specific]

3/ [Second point — build on the first]

...

[Last tweet]/ [Takeaway or summary + optional CTA]
```

Good for: In-depth takes, step-by-step content, comprehensive arguments.

### Thread: Story
```
1/ [Specific moment — put the reader in the scene]

2/ [Context — what led here]

3-8/ [The journey — conflict, turns, discoveries]

Last/ [The lesson — what you now believe because of this experience]
```

Good for: Building personal brand, humanizing the company, memorable content.

## Call-to-Action Patterns

- **Discussion:** "Agree or disagree?" / "What am I missing?"
- **Thread tease:** "Here's the breakdown 🧵" / "Full thread:"
- **Resource:** "I wrote up the full guide — linked below." / "DM me for the template."
- **Repost ask:** "RT if this would help someone you know."

## Things to Avoid on X

- **Overlong single tweets** — if it needs >280 chars, make it a thread with a proper hook
- **Hedging every take** — "In my opinion, it could be argued that perhaps..." kills engagement
