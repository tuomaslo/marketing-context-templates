## Subject Line Patterns That Work

The subject line is 90% of the email's success. Patterns that perform for us:

| Pattern | Example | When to use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Specific number | "7 hooks that doubled our LinkedIn reach" | Educational content |
| The direct question | "Are you making this content mistake?" | Pain-point content |
| The "how to" | "How [Customer] cut content production from 8h to 90 min" | Case study / proof |
| Curiosity gap | "The content problem nobody talks about" | Opinion / insight |
| Personal / direct | "[First name], this took us 3 months to figure out" | Nurture / relationship |
| Product news | "[Feature] is live — here's what it does" | Product updates |
| [Your pattern] | [Example] | [When] |

**Preview text rule:** Always set it — it's a second subject line. Should expand on the subject, not repeat it.

## Email Structure Templates

### Newsletter
```
Subject: [Specific, benefit-clear subject]
Preview: [Expands on subject — don't just repeat it]

[BODY]

[Opening: 1–2 sentences — hook or context for this issue]

[Main section header]
[Content — 2–4 paragraphs or a structured list]

[Optional secondary section — shorter]

[CTA — one clear action; no competing links]

[Sign-off — personal, brief]
[Your name / brand sign-off]

[Footer — unsubscribe + address]
```

### Product Update Email
```
Subject: [Feature name] is live
Preview: [What you can now do with it]

[BODY]

[One-line hook: what changed]

[The before/after — what was painful, what's better now]

[1–3 key things to know — bullet points]

[CTA button: "Try it now" / "See what's new"]

[Optional: quote from a beta user]

[Brief sign-off]
```

### Nurture Email (Day 1 — Welcome)
```
Subject: Welcome — here's where to start
Preview: [The one thing to do in the next 5 minutes]

[BODY]

[Warm, direct welcome — who you are and why this matters]

[What they should do first — single, specific action]

[What to expect from future emails]

[Quick CTA]
```

## Things to Avoid in Email

- **"We're excited to announce"** → Just announce it. Lead with the what, not the emotion.
- **Multiple competing CTAs** → One email, one action. More than one = decision paralysis = no clicks.
- **Generic subject lines** → "Our March Newsletter" → tells them nothing and earns nothing.
- **Long paragraphs** → 3 sentences max per paragraph in email. People skim.
- **Sending without preview text set** → Wastes the second subject line.
