## Call-to-Action Patterns

What we ask people to do at the end of posts — pick what fits the content:

- **Discussion:** "What would you add?" / "Disagree? Tell me why."
- **Engagement:** "Drop a 🔥 if this resonates." / "Tag someone who needs to see this."
- **Resource:** "I wrote up the full playbook — link in comments." / "DM me '[keyword]' and I'll send it."
- **Follow:** "Following for more on [topic]." / "[Content series] drops every [day]."

## Format Templates

### Thought leadership post structure:
1. Hook (1 line — scroll-stopper)
2. Setup (2–3 lines — expand the claim or tease the insight)
3. Body (5–8 bullet points or short paragraphs — the actual value)
4. Takeaway (1–2 lines — the so-what)
5. CTA (1 line — light touch)

### Story post structure:
1. Hook (surprising or specific moment)
2. Context (what was the situation)
3. What happened (the conflict or journey)
4. What I learned (the insight — this is why they read it)
5. Application (how they can use it)

---

## Things to Avoid on LinkedIn

- **Link posts in the main post text** — LinkedIn suppresses these; put links in comments
- **Walls of text** — break every 2–3 lines
- **[Your specific mistake 1]** — [Why]
- **[Your specific mistake 2]** — [Why]
