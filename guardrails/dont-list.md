## Tone Mistakes to Avoid

### Hype without substance
❌ "This is INCREDIBLE and will TRANSFORM how you work!!!"
✅ Show why it's good. What changed? What's the before and after?

### Corporate distance
❌ "We are pleased to inform our valued customers that..."
✅ Talk like a human. "Here's what changed, and why it matters."

### Hedging everything
❌ "This may potentially be helpful for some users in certain situations..."
✅ Make a claim. If you're not sure, say why — don't just soften everything to mush.

### Forced enthusiasm
❌ Excessive exclamation marks!!! Every sentence!!! So exciting!!!
✅ One exclamation point per email, max. Earn the enthusiasm with the content.

### Passive voice (habitual)
❌ "Mistakes were made." / "The feature was shipped by the team."
✅ "We made a mistake." / "We shipped [feature]." Own it.

### Vague time references
❌ "Recently", "soon", "in the near future", "down the road"
✅ Specific dates, versions, or timeframes — or acknowledge you can't say yet

## Formatting Rules

Content structure mistakes that make output harder to use:

- **No walls of text** — max 3 sentences per paragraph in digital content
- **No jargon without explanation** — if we use a technical term, briefly say what it means the first time
- **No bulleted lists of 10+ items** without grouping or hierarchy — it's overwhelming
- **No all-caps for emphasis** — use bold or italics
- **No passive CTAs** — "Learn more" → say what they'll learn; "Click here" → say where and why
